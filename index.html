About AWT

The Arewa Women for Tinubu is a pan Northern Nigeria collective of grassroot political women from all the Nineteen (19) Northern States that have been working tirelessly in promoting Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu among our contemporaries in the North and in extension Nigeria. The Group also seeks, advocate for women inclusiveness in governance and support all developmental endeavors that enhance women participation in politics.
 
The Arewa Women for Tinubu (AWT) is the cauldron of politically minded activists drawn from the Trade and Labour Organizations, Youth Organization, Market Women Associations, Community - Based Associations, Faith Based Associations, Issue - Based Organizations, Socio-Cultural Organizations as well as individual Women Political Leaders, Opinion Leaders, Mobilisers and Women Activists.

AWT is vast Women Support Group. We represent the women, from all the Nineteen (19) states across Northern part of our great Country Nigeria. From the North-Eastern States of Adamawa, Borno, Bauchi, Gombe, Yobe and Taraba; to the North-West Region comprising Kaduna, Kano, Jigawa, Katsina, Kebbi, Sokoto and Zamfara and to the North Central States of Benue, Kogi, Nassarawa, Plateau, Niger, Kwara and the Federal Capital Territory ‚Äì Arewa Women For Tinubu is rooted. FAQ on Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu

Key Information

Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu was born on 29 March 1952 in Osun State. His mother, Hajia Abibatu Mogaji, was a trader that later became the Iyaloja of Lagos

His Education?

He attended St. John's Primary School, Aroloya, Lagos and Children's Home School in Ibadan. He proceeded to the United States in 1975. He gained admission into Richard J. Daley College in Chicago, Illinois. Thereafter he went to Chicago State University and graduated in 1979 with a Bachelor of Science Degree in Accounting.

His Early Career?

Tinubu worked for the American companies such as Arthur Andersen, Deloitte, Haskins, & Sells, and GTE Services Corporation, after returning to Nigeria in 1983, Bola Tinubu joined Mobil Oil Nigeria, and later became an executive of the Company

His Early Political Career

Tinubu‚Äôs political career began in 1992, when he joined the Social Democratic Party (SDP)and got elected into the Senate, representing the Lagos West Constituency in the short-lived Nigerian Third Republic.

After the results of the 12 June 1993 Presidential Elections were annulled, Tinubu became a founding member of the Prodemocracy National Democratic Coalition (NADECO), a group which mobilized support for the restoration of democracy and recognition of Moshood Abiola as winner of the 12 June election.After the military took over power in 1993, he went into exile in 1994 and returned in 1998 following the death of General Sani Abacha,the then Head of State.

In the run-up to the 1999 elections, Bola Tinubu was a prot√©g√© of Alliance for Democracy (AD) leaders Abraham Adesanya and Ayo Adebanjo. He was elected the Executive Governor of Lagos State on the platform of Alliance For Democracy (AD).

As Governor of Lagos

Tinubu assumed office in May 1999, he built mass housing units for the poor. During the eight-year period of his tenure, he made large investments in education. He also constructed new roads required to meet the needs of the fast-growing population of the State.

Tinubu, was re-elected into office as governor in April 2003. His reflection was seen as a great political feat as all other States in the South West fell to Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) controlled Federal Government.

Tinubu has been widely perceived as the "Godfather of Lagos", leveraging his political capital in the mega city-state to influence regional and national affairs.

Tinubu was active in negotiations to bring together the fragmented opposition parties into a "mega-party" capable of challenging the PDP. In February 2013. The negotiations in creating a "mega opposition" party became paid off with the merger of Nigeria's three biggest opposition parties ‚Äì the Action Congress of Nigeria (ACN), the Congress for Progressive Change (CPC), the All Nigeria Peoples Party (ANPP), a faction of the All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA) and the new PDP (nPDP), a faction of serving governors of the then ruling People's Democratic Party ‚Äì into the All Progressives Congress (APC).

In 2015, Tinubu supported General Muhammadu Buhari, who won the Presidential election. He also played a major role in nominating the Vice President Professor Pastor Yemi Osibanjo, his ally and former Commissioner of Justice.

Tinubu has gone on to play an important role in the Buhari administration, supporting government policies and holding onto the internal party reins. He also supported Buhari's reelection in 2019.
